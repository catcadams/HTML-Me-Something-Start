My 2023 Summer Trip to Michigan

Last summer, I visited the western side of Michigan with my boyfriend. We went to a bunch of different towns while we were there to really experience it all. The beaches are beautiful, there are fun lighthouses, great coffee shops, and a wide variety of breweries!

Here are all the towns we visited:

  • Yankee Springs State Park
  • Holland
  • Saugatuck and Douglas
  • Grand Haven
  • Grand Rapids
  • Fennville
  • South Haven
  • Kalamazoo

Yankee Springs State Park

After an awful drive through pouring down rain, we made it to our first location of the week: Yankee Springs State Park. We were at a campsite very close to Gun Lake, one of the nine lakes located within the park boundaries, and got to watch a beautiful sunset over the lake!

Holland

In Holland, we went to 3 different breweries: New Holland Brewing, Our Brewing Company, and Big Lake Brewing. We also visited our first beach and lighthouse of the week there! It was too cold to get in the water, but it made for a beautiful walk.

Saugatuck and Douglas

Saugatuck and Douglas are very close to each other, so it's easy to tour the two at the same time. We went up Mount Baldhead in Saugatuck, which is a large dune, then came down the other side to our second beach of the trip, Oval Beach. Before leaving the area, we made sure to swing by the local brewery, Saugutuck Brewing CO!

Grand Haven

We drove up to Grand Haven to check out another beach and lighthouse; this was a short visit, but we were able to grab a drink at Odd Side Brewery before heading out.

Grand Rapids

Grand Rapids was one of my favorite stops; it reminds me of St. Louis in the way that it's a decent sized city but has a small town feel to it. In the suburbs of Grand Rapids, there is a gorgeous park called the Frederik Meijer Gardens & Sculpter Park; the park holds so many fascinating works of art, several different horticultural displays, a beautiful Japanese garden, and even a carnivorous plant house! This place is absolutely worth a visit. Of course, we made sure to check out a couple of breweries while we were in Grand Rapids: Founder's, Jolly Pumpkin, and (my favorite) Brewery Vivant.

Fennville

We were only in Fennville for an evening, grabbing a delicious dinner at Salt of the Earth. It was the best meal we had the entire week and I hope that I get to go back there someday soon!

Here is the link to the Salt of the Earth website! Salt of the Earth Restaurant

South Haven

I specifically wanted to go to South Haven to attend the National Blueberry Festival; it was happening over the course of 3 days and we were only able to attend the first day, but it was a really fun experience. It extended over the boardwalk down to the beach and lighthouse so we got to walk the beach, check out the farmer's market they hosted, bought some blueberries, blueberry jam, and blueberry bbq sauce. The only thing we missed out on was the blueberry pancake breakfast. We also went to a super cute, alien themed coffee shop called The Flying Saucer and made sure to try a local brewery, which was called Three Blondes Brewery.

Here is the link to this years Blueberry Festival! National Blueberry Festival 2024

Kalamazoo

Kalamazoo felt like an extra special visit as that is where my boyfriend went to college and he hadn't been back since his school days. We got to walk around the town and check out two really cool local breweries, Bell's Brewery, one of the first craft breweries, and One Well, an arcade brewery. We ended our trip to Michigan playing pinball and plotting out when we would be able to come back!
